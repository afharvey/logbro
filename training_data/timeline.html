Silent Failure — Filesystem Dead, Kubernetes Unaware

⚡ The EXT4 journal is permanently aborted. All writes to /dev/sdg fail with error -5 (EIO). The htree_dirblock_to_tree inode #655361 error -5 message fires every ~15 seconds for the entire duration. Although /dev/sdh (same LUN 5) was re-enumerated at 22:34:55, the EXT4 volume mounted on the old /dev/sdg path is not remounted — the filesystem remains in the aborted state. VolumeAttachment reports attached: true throughout. No CSI driver errors surface.

Detection Gap

Jan 15 22:28:36 First failure signal — Aborting journal on device sdg-8
Jan 16 16:24:44 Latest confirmed failure activity in logs (Detected aborted journal in kern.log); no resolution events observed
≥ 17h 56m Minimum confirmed silent failure window visible in logs

Why was this not detected sooner? The Kubernetes VolumeAttachment object continued to report attached: true throughout — the Azure Disk CSI driver does not poll filesystem health, only block device attachment state. The disk (LUN 5) was re-enumerated as /dev/sdh at 22:34:55, which the CSI driver may have interpreted as a successful reattach, even though the EXT4 journal on the mounted path /dev/sdg was permanently aborted. Detection likely relied on application-level errors from Neo4j (the neo4j.DatabaseR and neo4j.RaftLogPr processes visible in the kernel logs at the moment of failure) eventually triggering a Raft panic or availability alert. The exact human detection time is not present in the kernel logs provided.
